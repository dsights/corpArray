FEMA Compliance Updates

Posted on August 9, 2025 by CorpArray

The Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) is the cornerstone of India's foreign exchange regime. Enacted to facilitate external trade and payments and to promote the orderly development and maintenance of the foreign exchange market in India, FEMA is a dynamic piece of legislation that is constantly evolving. For businesses engaged in cross-border transactions, staying abreast of the latest FEMA compliance updates is not just a matter of good practice; it is a legal necessity.

In this article, we will delve into some of the recent key updates and their implications for businesses. We will also provide practical guidance on how to ensure ongoing compliance with the ever-changing regulatory landscape.

Recent Amendments to the Foreign Exchange Management (Non-debt Instruments) Rules, 2019

The Non-debt Instruments (NDI) Rules are a critical component of the FEMA framework, governing all foreign investments in the equity of Indian companies. The government has recently introduced several significant amendments to these rules, aimed at further liberalizing the foreign investment regime and promoting ease of doing business.

One of the most notable changes is the increase in the sectoral cap for foreign investment in the insurance sector from 49% to 74%. This is a landmark reform that is expected to attract significant foreign capital into the insurance industry, leading to increased competition and innovation.

Overseas Investment by Indian Residents

The RBI has recently rationalized the regulatory framework for overseas investments by Indian residents, consolidating the existing regulations into a single, comprehensive framework. The new framework, which comprises the Foreign Exchange Management (Overseas Investment) Rules, 2022, and the Foreign Exchange Management (Overseas Investment) Regulations, 2022, is designed to simplify the existing rules and make it easier for Indian businesses to invest abroad.

Some of the key changes include a more liberal regime for overseas investments by listed Indian companies, a simplified process for obtaining approvals, and greater clarity on the valuation norms for overseas investments.

Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

The LRS allows resident individuals to remit up to USD 250,000 per financial year for any permitted current or capital account transaction. The RBI has recently clarified certain aspects of the LRS, including the documentation requirements and the process for making remittances. It is important for individuals to be aware of these clarifications to ensure that their remittances are compliant with the regulations.

Practical Guidance for Businesses

Given the dynamic nature of the FEMA regulations, it is essential for businesses to have a robust compliance framework in place. Here are some practical tips to help you stay on top of your FEMA compliance obligations:

  • Stay Informed: Regularly monitor the websites of the RBI and the Ministry of Finance for the latest updates and circulars.
  • Seek Expert Advice: Engage a qualified professional to provide expert advice on all your FEMA-related matters.
  • Maintain Proper Documentation: Ensure that you maintain proper documentation for all your foreign exchange transactions.
  • Conduct Regular Audits: Conduct regular internal audits to identify and rectify any compliance gaps.

Conclusion

FEMA compliance is a complex and challenging area, but it is also a critical aspect of doing business in India. By staying informed, seeking expert advice, and maintaining a robust compliance framework, you can ensure that your business is always on the right side of the law.
