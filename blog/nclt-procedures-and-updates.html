NCLT Procedures and Updates

Posted on August 9, 2025 by CorpArray

The National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) is a quasi-judicial body in India that adjudicates issues relating to Indian companies. The NCLT was established under the Companies Act, 2013 and has wide powers to deal with a variety of corporate matters, including insolvency and bankruptcy, mergers and acquisitions, and oppression and mismanagement.

In this article, we will provide an overview of the procedures and recent updates related to the NCLT.

NCLT Procedures

The procedures for filing an application with the NCLT are governed by the National Company Law Tribunal Rules, 2016. The first step is to file a petition or an application with the appropriate bench of the NCLT. The petition or application should be accompanied by all the relevant documents and a fee.

Once the petition or application is filed, the NCLT will issue a notice to the other party, who will be given an opportunity to file a reply. The NCLT will then hear both the parties and pass an order.

Recent Updates

1. Introduction of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)

The IBC was enacted in 2016 to provide a time-bound and market-driven process for the resolution of insolvency and bankruptcy. The NCLT is the adjudicating authority for all matters under the IBC. The IBC has been a game-changer in the Indian corporate landscape, and it has helped to improve the credit culture and to reduce the time taken for the resolution of insolvency.

2. E-filing of documents

The NCLT has introduced a system of e-filing of documents, which has made it easier and more convenient for parties to file their petitions and applications. The e-filing system has also helped to reduce the use of paper and to make the NCLT a more environmentally friendly organization.

3. Video conferencing

The NCLT has started to conduct hearings through video conferencing, which has been a great help during the COVID-19 pandemic. Video conferencing has helped to ensure that the NCLT continues to function even during the lockdown, and it has also made it easier for parties to attend hearings from the comfort of their homes or offices.

Conclusion

The NCLT is a vital institution in the Indian corporate landscape. It plays a key role in ensuring that corporate disputes are resolved in a timely and efficient manner.
