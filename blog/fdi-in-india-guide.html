FDI in India: A Comprehensive Guide

Posted on August 9, 2025 by CorpArray

India has emerged as one of the most attractive destinations for foreign direct investment (FDI) in the world. The government has taken a number of steps to liberalize the FDI policy and to create a more investor-friendly environment. As a result, India has seen a significant increase in FDI inflows in recent years.

In this article, we will provide a comprehensive guide to the FDI policy in India, including the entry routes, sectoral caps, and compliance requirements.

Entry Routes for FDI

There are two main entry routes for FDI in India:

  • Automatic Route: Under this route, no prior approval is required from the government for FDI. The investor only needs to inform the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) within 30 days of the investment.
  • Government Route: Under this route, prior approval is required from the government for FDI. The application for approval has to be made to the concerned ministry or department.

Sectoral Caps

The government has specified sectoral caps for FDI in various sectors. These caps range from 26% to 100%. In some sectors, FDI is prohibited altogether.

Some of the key sectors and their FDI caps are as follows:

  • Agriculture: 100% under the automatic route
  • Defence: 74% under the automatic route and up to 100% under the government route
  • Insurance: 74% under the automatic route
  • Retail: 100% under the automatic route for single-brand retail and 51% under the government route for multi-brand retail

Compliance Requirements

There are a number of compliance requirements that need to be met by foreign investors in India. These include:

  • Reporting of FDI: All FDI inflows have to be reported to the RBI within 30 days.
  • Pricing Guidelines: The pricing of the shares issued to foreign investors has to be in accordance with the pricing guidelines prescribed by the RBI.
  • Annual Return on Foreign Liabilities and Assets (FLA): All Indian companies that have received FDI have to file an annual return on FLA with the RBI.

Conclusion

The FDI policy in India is constantly evolving. It is important for foreign investors to stay abreast of the latest changes to ensure that they are in compliance with the law.
